Graphics

A picture is worth a thousand words. Still or motion, IGT imagery should depict real people in real situations in real environments. Never contrived, trite, or clichéd, IGT imagery embodies a photojournalistic eye to capture pure moments in the lives of our customers and players, and their interactions with one another.

Our Graphic Element

Singly or together, the rings bring harmony to our photography and type, signaling IGT’s continued pursuit of innovation. Unabashed, unconstrained, and purposeful, the rings surround and fuse into our imagery becoming a natural part of our message.

Graphic 1: Focus

Employing a single ring provides focus, bringing precedence to our products and messaging

Graphic 2: Combined

Combining the rings together, this playful variation signals connectivity and collaboration. Incorporating our bold contrasting brand colors.

Graphic 3: Integrated

Fusing the rings together with imagery yields a further distinctive expression that suggests convergence.

Design Principles

Visual Reference

Intended to be an evolution from previous IGT branding and a derivative of the IGT logo, this new graphic expression pays homage to IGT’s history. By simply activating the rings—moving and scaling the rings — you can transform the graphic element into a new dynamic form that conveys a strong sense of movement, energy, and depth.

Integrate

To convert the graphic expression into a single integrated shape, convert the shape into a single compound path and fill with a gradient. Use this graphic form against white space or infused within a gradient background. Create additional depth by incorporating an additional rings above. You may use a compound path to mask an image as well.

Move or Combine

To create a sense of energy and depth, scale the rings. To better balance, rotate the rings in different directions. To add intensity and impact, multiply, overlap, and combine the rings. However you express it, be sure to maintain a good sense of balance.

Graphic Element Don’ts

Don’t impede the visibility of game screens. This is especially true when working with licensed properties

Don’t apply arbitrary effects to the rings

Don’t apply the ring as halo, specifically when using lifestyle imagery

Don’t arbitrarily apply ring to photography or illogically intersect its shown subjects

Don’t arbitrarily place atop imagery

Don’t interfere with the IGT logo

Don’t disrupt the readability of headlines

Don’t use rings as an infographic element

Don’t use rings as a background treatment adornment
