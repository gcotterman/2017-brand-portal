Typography

In conversation, tone is everything. It gives words subtlety and reinforces the intended meaning. When dealing with the written word, presented on the printed page or on the digital display, the choice of typeface is equally important. It gives the message a distinct personality and character.

Verlag Type Family

Type matters. It is more than a design element, articulating our message, expressing what we say and how we say it. Modern, easy to read, and humanist, the Verlag is closely aligned with the IGT brand personality. We use it within products and in communications across brands.

  • Use Verlag
  • Keep it simple
  • Limit type sizes to three
  • Keep it flush left
  • Use sentence case

Leading and Tracking

8 pt. / leading: 12 pt . / tracking: 0

10 pt. / leading: 13 pt . / tracking: 0

20 pt. / leading: 22 pt . / tracking: 0

All leading is not equal. As a general rule of thumb: the smaller the point size, the larger the leading; the higher the point size, the tighter the leading. Leading should be set between 100 to 150 percent of the point size. The tracking for Verlag can generally be set to 0. Adjust tracking and kerning where needed based on application.

Type can lend clarity to your message. By employing a consistent type hierarchy, the readability of your communication becomes more clear and meaningful. The examples above give some guidance on type hierarchy. In general, maintain a balanced contrast between titles/subtitles and body copy.
